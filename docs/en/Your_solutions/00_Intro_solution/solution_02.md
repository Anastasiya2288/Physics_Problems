# Systems of Equations

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. System of Linear Equations

A **system of linear equations** is a collection of two or more linear equations involving the same variables.  
A solution to the system is a set of values for the variables that satisfies **all equations simultaneously**.

For two variables $x$ and $y$, a general linear system can be written as

$$
a_1x+b_1y=c_1
$$

$$
a_2x+b_2y=c_2
$$

where $a_1,a_2,b_1,b_2$ are coefficients and $c_1,c_2$ are constants.

---

### 2. Substitution Method

The **substitution method** is one of the most common ways to solve systems of equations.

The steps are:

1. Solve one equation for one variable.
2. Substitute this expression into the second equation.
3. Solve the resulting equation for the remaining variable.
4. Substitute the result back to find the other variable.

This method works well when one equation can easily isolate a variable.

---

### 3. Checking the Solution

A correct solution must satisfy **both original equations**.  
After finding the values of $x$ and $y$, they should be substituted back into the original equations to verify correctness.

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

We are given the system

$$
2x+3y=12
$$

$$
x-y=1
$$

---

# Step 1: Solve One Equation for One Variable

The second equation is easier to rearrange:

$$
x-y=1
$$

Solve for $x$ by adding $y$ to both sides:

$$
x=1+y
$$

This expression will now be substituted into the first equation.

---

# Step 2: Substitute into the First Equation

The first equation is

$$
2x+3y=12
$$

Substitute $x=1+y$:

$$
2\cdot\left(1+y\right)+3y=12
$$

---

# Step 3: Simplify the Equation

First distribute the $2$:

$$
2+2y+3y=12
$$

Combine like terms:

$$
2+5y=12
$$

Now subtract $2$ from both sides:

$$
5y=10
$$

Divide both sides by $5$:

$$
y=2
$$

---

# Step 4: Substitute Back to Find $x$

Recall the earlier expression:

$$
x=1+y
$$

Substitute $y=2$:

$$
x=1+2
$$

$$
x=3
$$

---

# Step 5: Verify the Solution

Substitute the values into the original equations.

### First equation

$$
2x+3y=2\cdot3+3\cdot2
$$

$$
=6+6
$$

$$
=12
$$

The first equation is satisfied.

---

### Second equation

$$
x-y=3-2
$$

$$
=1
$$

The second equation is also satisfied.

---

# Final Answer

$$
x=3
$$

$$
y=2
$$

The pair $3,2$ satisfies both equations simultaneously.
