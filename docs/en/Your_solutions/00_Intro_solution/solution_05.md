# Trigonometry and Vector Components

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Vector

A **vector** is a quantity that has both **magnitude** and **direction**.  
In two-dimensional space, a vector can be represented using its **horizontal component** and **vertical component**.

If a vector has magnitude $A$ and forms an angle $\theta$ with the horizontal axis, it can be decomposed into two perpendicular components.

---

### 2. Vector Components

The horizontal and vertical components of a vector are calculated using trigonometric functions.

Horizontal component:

$$
A_x=A\cos\theta
$$

Vertical component:

$$
A_y=A\sin\theta
$$

where

- $A$ is the magnitude of the vector  
- $\theta$ is the angle with the horizontal axis  

---

### 3. Trigonometric Values for Special Angles

For common angles we often know the exact trigonometric values.

For $\theta=60^\circ$:

$$
\cos60^\circ=\frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
\sin60^\circ=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

These values allow us to compute vector components exactly.

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

We are given:

Magnitude of the vector

$$
A=15
$$

Angle with the horizontal axis

$$
\theta=60^\circ
$$

We need to calculate:

- Horizontal component $A_x$
- Vertical component $A_y$

---

# Step 1: Calculate the Horizontal Component

Using the formula

$$
A_x=A\cos\theta
$$

Substitute the known values:

$$
A_x=15\cos60^\circ
$$

Using the trigonometric value

$$
\cos60^\circ=\frac{1}{2}
$$

So

$$
A_x=15\cdot\frac{1}{2}
$$

$$
A_x=7.5
$$

---

# Step 2: Calculate the Vertical Component

Using the formula

$$
A_y=A\sin\theta
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
A_y=15\sin60^\circ
$$

Using the trigonometric identity

$$
\sin60^\circ=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

So

$$
A_y=15\cdot\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

$$
A_y=\frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}
$$

Numerical approximation:

$$
\sqrt{3}\approx1.732
$$

$$
A_y\approx12.99
$$

---

# Final Answer

Horizontal component:

$$
A_x=7.5
$$

Vertical component:

$$
A_y=\frac{15\sqrt{3}}{2}\approx12.99
$$
