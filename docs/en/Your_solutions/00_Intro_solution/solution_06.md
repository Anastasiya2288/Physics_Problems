# Function Analysis: Local Maximum and Minimum

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Function

A **function** is a mathematical relationship that assigns each input value $x$ exactly one output value $f(x)$.

In this problem we analyze the quadratic function

$$
f(x)=3x^2-12x+7
$$

Quadratic functions generally form a **parabola**.

---

### 2. Local Maximum and Local Minimum

A **local maximum** is a point where the function value is greater than nearby values.

A **local minimum** is a point where the function value is smaller than nearby values.

For quadratic functions:

- If the coefficient of $x^2$ is **positive**, the parabola opens **upward** and has a **minimum**.
- If the coefficient of $x^2$ is **negative**, the parabola opens **downward** and has a **maximum**.

---

### 3. Vertex of a Quadratic Function

For a quadratic function

$$
f(x)=ax^2+bx+c
$$

the $x$-coordinate of the vertex is

$$
x_v=-\frac{b}{2a}
$$

The corresponding $y$-value is obtained by substituting $x_v$ into the function:

$$
y_v=f(x_v)
$$

The vertex represents either the **maximum or minimum point** of the parabola.

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

We are given the function

$$
f(x)=3x^2-12x+7
$$

From the standard form

$$
f(x)=ax^2+bx+c
$$

we identify the coefficients:

$$
a=3
$$

$$
b=-12
$$

$$
c=7
$$

---

# Step 1: Determine Whether the Parabola Opens Upward or Downward

The coefficient of $x^2$ is

$$
a=3>0
$$

Since $a$ is positive, the parabola **opens upward**.

Therefore, the function has a **local minimum** and **no local maximum**.

---

# Step 2: Find the $x$-Coordinate of the Vertex

Using the vertex formula

$$
x_v=-\frac{b}{2a}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
x_v=-\frac{-12}{2\cdot3}
$$

$$
x_v=\frac{12}{6}
$$

$$
x_v=2
$$

---

# Step 3: Find the Corresponding $y$-Value

Now substitute $x=2$ into the function.

$$
f(x)=3x^2-12x+7
$$

$$
f(2)=3\cdot2^2-12\cdot2+7
$$

First compute the square:

$$
2^2=4
$$

Now substitute:

$$
f(2)=3\cdot4-24+7
$$

$$
=12-24+7
$$

$$
=-5
$$

---

# Step 4: Identify the Extremum Point

The vertex of the parabola is

$$
(2,-5)
$$

Since the parabola opens upward, this point represents the **local minimum**.

---

# Final Result

Local minimum:

$$
x=2
$$

$$
f(2)=-5
$$

Minimum point:

$$
(2,-5)
$$

The function has **no local maximum** because the parabola opens upward.
