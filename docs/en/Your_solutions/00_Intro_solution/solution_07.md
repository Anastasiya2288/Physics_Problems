# Logic & Series: Fly Between Bicycle and Wall

## Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Distance–Speed–Time Relationship

In kinematics, the basic relationship between distance, speed, and time is

$$
d=vt
$$

where

- $d$ is distance  
- $v$ is velocity (speed)  
- $t$ is time  

This formula allows us to determine how far an object travels if we know its speed and the time of motion.

---

### 2. Relative Motion

Relative motion describes how objects move with respect to each other.

If one object moves toward another, the time until they meet can be determined using their speeds and the distance between them.

---

### 3. Infinite Series in Motion Problems

Sometimes problems involve many repeated movements (like the fly flying back and forth infinitely many times).

Instead of calculating every trip, we can look for a **simpler physical insight**: determine the **total time the motion lasts**, then compute the distance traveled using the fly's speed.

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

We are given:

Distance between the bicycle and the wall

$$
d=10\text{ m}
$$

Speed of the bicycle

$$
v_b=1\text{ m/s}
$$

Speed of the fly

$$
v_f=2\text{ m/s}
$$

The fly keeps flying back and forth between the bicycle and the wall until the bicycle reaches the wall.

---

# Step 1: Determine the Time Until the Bicycle Reaches the Wall

The bicycle travels toward the wall with speed

$$
v_b=1\text{ m/s}
$$

The distance to the wall is

$$
d=10\text{ m}
$$

Using the motion formula

$$
t=\frac{d}{v}
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
t=\frac{10}{1}
$$

$$
t=10\text{ s}
$$

So the bicycle reaches the wall in **10 seconds**.

---

# Step 2: Determine How Long the Fly is Flying

The fly starts immediately and continues flying until the bicycle reaches the wall.

Therefore the fly is flying for

$$
t=10\text{ s}
$$

even though it changes direction many times.

---

# Step 3: Calculate the Total Distance the Fly Travels

The speed of the fly is

$$
v_f=2\text{ m/s}
$$

Using the distance formula

$$
d=vt
$$

Substitute the values:

$$
d=2\cdot10
$$

$$
d=20\text{ m}
$$

---

# Final Result

The fly travels a total distance of

$$
20\text{ m}
$$

before being crushed when the bicycle reaches the wall.
