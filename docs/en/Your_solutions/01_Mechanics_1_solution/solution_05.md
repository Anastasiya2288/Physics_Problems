# Relative Velocity — Theory + Solution

---

# Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Relative Velocity

The velocity of the boat relative to the ground is:

$$
\vec{v}_{bg}=\vec{v}_{bw}+\vec{v}_{wg}
$$

where:
- $\vec{v}_{bw}$ — boat relative to water
- $\vec{v}_{wg}$ — water (river) relative to ground

---

### 2. Condition for Moving Straight North

To move **directly north**, the horizontal (east-west) component of total velocity must be zero:

$$
v_{x,\text{total}}=0
$$

---

### 3. Components of Velocity

If the boat is aimed at angle $\theta$ **west of north**, then:

$$
v_{bw,x}=-v_b\sin\theta
$$

$$
v_{bw,y}=v_b\cos\theta
$$

---

### 4. Time to Cross

Time depends only on vertical component:

$$
t=\frac{\text{width}}{v_y}
$$

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

Given:

$$
v_{\text{river}}=2\ \text{m/s (east)}
$$

$$
v_b=5\ \text{m/s}
$$

$$
\text{width}=200\ \text{m}
$$

---

# Step 1. Condition for Straight North Motion

Total horizontal velocity must be zero:

$$
v_{bw,x}+v_{\text{river}}=0
$$

Substitute:

$$
-5\sin\theta+2=0
$$

---

# Step 2. Solve for Angle

$$
5\sin\theta=2
$$

$$
\sin\theta=\frac{2}{5}=0.4
$$

$$
\theta=\arcsin0.4\approx23.6^\circ
$$

---

# Step 3. Interpretation

The boat must aim:

$$
23.6^\circ\ \text{west of north}
$$

to cancel the river current.

---

# Step 4. Vertical Velocity

$$
v_y=5\cos\theta
$$

$$
\cos23.6^\circ\approx0.917
$$

$$
v_y\approx5\cdot0.917=4.585\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

# Step 5. Time to Cross

$$
t=\frac{200}{4.585}
$$

$$
t\approx43.6\ \text{s}
$$

---

# Final Answers

### Direction:

$$
\theta\approx23.6^\circ\ \text{west of north}
$$

---

### Time to cross:

$$
t\approx43.6\ \text{s}
$$

---

# Comments

- The boat must aim upstream to cancel the river drift.
- Only the vertical component determines crossing time.
- Stronger current → larger angle required.
