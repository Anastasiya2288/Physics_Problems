# Circular Motion — Theory + Solution

---

# Useful Definitions and Formulas

### 1. Centripetal Acceleration

For motion in a circle:

$$
a_c=\frac{v^2}{R}
$$

or using angular velocity:

$$
a_c=\omega^2R
$$

---

### 2. Angular Velocity

Angular velocity is:

$$
\omega=\frac{2\pi}{T}
$$

where:
- $T$ — period of rotation

---

### 3. Relation Between Linear and Angular Velocity

$$
v=\omega R
$$

---

# Step-by-Step Solution

### Given:

$$
R=6378\ \text{km}=6.378\times10^6\ \text{m}
$$

Earth completes one rotation in:

$$
T=24\ \text{hours}=86400\ \text{s}
$$

---

# Step 1. Calculate Angular Velocity

$$
\omega=\frac{2\pi}{T}
$$

$$
\omega=\frac{2\pi}{86400}
$$

$$
\omega\approx7.27\times10^{-5}\ \text{rad/s}
$$

---

# Step 2. Calculate Centripetal Acceleration

Use:

$$
a_c=\omega^2R
$$

Substitute:

$$
a_c=(7.27\times10^{-5})^2\cdot6.378\times10^6
$$

---

### Compute:

$$
(7.27\times10^{-5})^2\approx5.29\times10^{-9}
$$

$$
a_c=5.29\times10^{-9}\cdot6.378\times10^6
$$

$$
a_c\approx0.0337\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

# Final Answer

$$
a_c\approx3.37\times10^{-2}\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

# Comments

- This acceleration is **very small** compared to gravity $g=9.8\ \text{m/s}^2$
- That’s why we don’t feel Earth's rotation.
- It slightly reduces effective weight at the equator.
